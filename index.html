Hi, I am Long-Jing (Claire)! I do research on Human-Robot Interaction.


I ask how technology can improve older adults' well-being and improve the relationship between older adults and their caregivers. Most recently, I have been working on how to co-design with older adults with dementia to improve older adults' purpose in life and build technology for their caregivers. I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Informatics at the Indiana University in Bloomington. My current advisors are Dr. Selma Šabanović and Dr. Christina Chung. I was advised by Dr. Hsiu-Ping Yueh and hold an MSc and BSc from National Taiwan University, where I looked at how social media foster older adults' social capital and well-being and investigated how older adults eat with a robot from a psychological perspective.

I invite you to pick a selected work below to understand more about me. You are welcome to email me your questions, comments, and critique.

Selected works

Robot as Eating Companion

Robots are the future of humans; we will be living with them when we get old. This is the study about a robot for commensality.

Social media

Social Media dominates human lives today. These are my studies on Facebook and Line on older adults in Taiwan.

Robot for plants

This is about a robot designed for plant caretakers.

The Food Diary App

Food is in existence with humans from past to present. But have we ever reflected on food? This is a study about how the food diary affects the well-being of the Baby Boomers.

A story about energy savers

Finding energy alternatives to fuel is essential for the future of society. This is a website project about a group of older adults on rooftop solar panels.
