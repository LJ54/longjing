Hi! I am Long-Jing (Claire)!

I do research in Human-Robot Interaction at the intersection of robotics, social computing, and food. I ask how technology can improve the relationship between older adults. I looked at how different social media foster the social capital and well-being of older adults. I have also investigated how older adults eat with a robot from a psychological perspective. Most recently, I am working on a co-design workshop on building a robot to improve older adults' purpose in life and building an art therapy robot for older adult caregivers.

I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in informatics at the Indiana University in Bloomington and holds an MSc and BSc from National Taiwan University.

Research

I study, design, and develop technologies that help older adults.

Robots

Robots are the future of humans; we will be living with them when we get old. These are the collection of studies about robots with older adults: Robots for commensality and robots for ikigai.

Social media

Social Media dominates human lives today. These are my studies on Facebook and Line on older adults in Taiwan.

Food

Food is in existence with humans from past to present. But have we ever reflected on food? This is a study about how the food diary affects the well-being of the Baby Boomers.

Others

Finding energy alternatives to fuel is essential for the future of society. This is a website project about a group of older adults on rooftop solar panels.
