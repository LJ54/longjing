Robot as Eating Companion

The Problem

Elderly who live alone and eat alone may have higher risks of psychological and physiological problems. A great number of social robotics for commensality have been created to solve this, but the question remains on whether these robots are good enough for the elders. In this paper, we created an eating robot prototype. We tested the prototype on 22 participants older than 55 and compared it with a stuffed animal.

Research methods

  • Survey to get a glance at levels of social presence, social support, and social facilitation from the robot.
  • Semi-structured interview to get an in-depth understanding of the eating experience.
  • Electroencephalogram (EEG) to measure participant’s emotions.

    • Design outcome

    Chuan Chuan （全全） is a robot embedded in the Zenbo robot interface. It gives an immediate response, nutrition information, say hi, and makes joke/ humor, and persuade older adults to do their own.

    Key Insights

  • Robots have a higher social presence, social support, and social facilitation than stuffed animals.
  • Participants especially like the robot with the characteristic of talking, remembering nutritional information, and can talk.
  • Participants expect a robot like a real eating companion.
  • Research method in emotion: using both EEG and Self-Assessment Manikin (SAM) of pre-eating and post-eating reflective emotions of older adults
  • Research method in mixed methods: longer duration of qualitative research

    • Robot to improve ikigai, the meaning of life (Research in progress)

    The Problem

    A feeling of purpose and meaning can improve lifespan, health, and general well-being in older persons. Today, we are trying to make older adults increase their well-being with the help of the robot.

    Research methods

  • Co-design workshop to design with older adults what they are interested in the robot.
  • Interview to get an understanding of what is the purpose or meaning of life.
  • Observation on elderly working with a robot
  • Survey to get understanding about what kind of voice is suitable for older adults

    • Robot to improve the relationship between caregiver and care recipient (Research in progress)

    The Problem

    Caregivers of older adults may sometimes be stressed and has trajectory decline health along with care recipient. This project is to create a robot to interact with caregivers and care recipients to work on art therapy to improve their relationships.

    Research methods

  • Co-design workshop to design with caregivers and care recipient.
  • Interview to get an understanding of the older adults caregiver.
