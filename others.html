Robot for Plants

Buzz! Deepening human connection to plants through technology (Work accepted for HRI 2022 Student Competition)

The Problem

In imagining a future where climate change forces more intimate relationships between humans and nature, social robots can be introduced to revolutionize the way humans understand and communicate with plants.

Research methods

Double Diamond design method with plant owners (Literature review, interviews, co-design workshops, design process)

Key Insights

Perspectives of the plant caretaking process: Positives (Plants give people feelings of home, participants also enjoyed the growth of plants and the day-to-day progress, plants also bring special connections to people, plants are good companions, practical reasons) and negatives (The distress of a lack of communication and killing a plant).

Design Outcome

A plant robot prototype and a story of an individual who interacts with a future version of our robot who overcomes the language barrier between plants and humans.

Roles

Researcher, Coordinator

Skills

UX research, Interviews, Co-design, Qualitative Analysis

Reflection

This was my first (soon to be) published project at Indiana University. The hardest thing in this project was learning and working with Arduino. But luckily, I had good friends where we work together to learn and debug the codes together. Lastly, we managed to make the desired robot as our participants anticipated in the co-design workshop.
I am grateful for my professor for teaching and guiding us throughout the process. And I am also grateful to the participants who joined our co-design workshops and gave thoughtful insights.
I had such a good time learning and co-designing this plant robot.
